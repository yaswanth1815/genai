The Taj Mahal is one of the most famous monuments in the world and a symbol of India’s rich architectural heritage. It was built by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal after her death in 1631. Constructed mainly from white marble, the monument is admired for its intricate carvings, beautiful gardens, and perfect symmetry. Located on the banks of the Yamuna River in Agra, the Taj Mahal attracts millions of visitors every year and is recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

The architecture of the Taj Mahal combines Persian, Islamic, and Indian styles, making it a masterpiece of Mughal engineering and design. Its large central dome, four surrounding minarets, and detailed floral patterns create a stunning visual appearance, especially during sunrise and moonlight. The monument not only represents eternal love but also showcases the artistic and cultural achievements of the Mughal era. Today, the Taj Mahal remains an important tourist destination and one of the Seven Wonders of the World.
